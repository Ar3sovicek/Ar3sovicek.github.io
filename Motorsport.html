BMW in motorsport

BMW has a long history of motorsport activities

Isle of Man TT

BMW has a long and successful history with the Isle of Man TT, one of the most prestigious motorcycle races in the world. Their involvement dates back to the 1930s, with a major milestone in 1939 when Georg Meier became the first non-British rider to win the Senior TT on a BMW. In recent years, BMW has made a strong comeback with bikes like the BMW S1000RR, achieving impressive results and multiple podium finishes. Riders such as Michael Dunlop have secured victories on BMWs, enhancing the brand's legacy at the TT. BMW uses the race to test new technologies, which later benefit their production motorcycles. Their ongoing participation underscores BMW's commitment to high-performance racing and respect for the tradition of the Isle of Man TT.

Dakar Rally

BMW has a strong history in the Dakar Rally, one of the toughest off-road races in the world. They started competing in the early 1980s with the BMW R80 G/S, winning their first race in 1981 with rider Hubert Auriol. BMW bikes, like the F650RR, continued to succeed, with Richard Sainct winning in 1999 and 2000. The challenging rally conditions have allowed BMW to develop and test new technologies, which are then used in their production motorcycles. These successes and innovations have cemented BMW's reputation for creating durable and high-performance off-road bikes.

Superbikes

BMW has made a big impact in superbike racing with their S1000RR model. Introduced in 2009, it's known for its powerful inline-four engine and advanced electronics like traction control and riding modes, setting new standards in performance and safety. The S1000RR has been successful in competitions like the Superbike World Championship, with riders achieving podium finishes and wins. Beyond racing, its technology has influenced BMW's other motorcycles, enhancing their performance and safety features. Overall, BMW's S1000RR showcases their commitment to high-performance motorcycles and innovation in the superbike category.

Formula Two

BMW has been involved in Formula Two through supplying engines to teams aiming for Formula One. Their engines have powered competitive cars, helping drivers advance in professional racing. This experience has also influenced BMW's development of high-performance engines for both racing and road cars, highlighting their commitment to motorsport and innovation.

Formula One

BMW competed in Formula One from 2006 to 2009 as the BMW Sauber team. They achieved podium finishes and showed their engineering skills in the highest level of racing. Although they withdrew later, their time in F1 left a lasting impact on automotive technology and highlighted BMW's commitment to performance and innovation.

Formula E

BMW competed in Formula E with the BMW i Andretti Motorsport team, focusing on electric racing and showcasing their expertise in electric vehicle technology. They achieved race wins and podium finishes, highlighting BMW's commitment to innovation in sustainable motorsport. Although they later withdrew, their participation contributed to advancing electric powertrains and reinforcing BMW's reputation in the world of electric racing.

Le Mans

BMW has a strong history at the 24 Hours of Le Mans race. They've won with different types of cars, including the memorable victory in 1999 with the BMW V12 LMR prototype. BMW's success shows their commitment to endurance racing and advanced engineering, making them a respected name in motorsport.
