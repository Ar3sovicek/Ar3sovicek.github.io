Executive sophistication

First generation

The first generation of the BMW 5 Series, called the E12, came out in 1972. It had a sleek design with round headlights and BMW's signature kidney grille. It offered different engine options, including some with fuel injection for better performance. People liked its balanced handling and advanced technology. It became known for blending luxury and sportiness, setting the stage for future 5 Series models.

1972 BMW 5 Series (E12)

1972-1981

BMW unveiled the first generation of the 5 Series in 1972, marking a significant step in re-establishing its status as a premium automaker.

In the 1970s, after overcoming financial struggles, BMW introduced the Garmisch concept, which laid the groundwork for the first generation 5 Series (E12). Designed by Paul Bracq and Marcello Gandini, the E12 used advanced technology, including computer simulations for safety. This model, released in 1972, was highly successful until replaced by the E28 in 1981.

The E12 featured a distinctive design with a four-headlight front, a broad grille, and a sporty look. It had a boxy profile with sleek lines, a raked windshield, and chrome accents. The interior included front bucket seats, a modern dashboard with a rectangular instrument cluster, and a rear bench for three passengers, though the middle seat was cramped due to the transmission tunnel.

The E12 came with four- and six-cylinder engines ranging from 90 PS to 184 PS. In 1980, BMW introduced the M535i with a powerful 218 PS 3.5-liter engine. Power was delivered to the rear wheels through various manual and automatic transmissions.

Second generation

The second generation of the BMW 5 Series, called the E28, came out in 1981 and lasted until 1988. It was an improved version of the first one, with a slightly bigger and more modern design. It had better engines, including petrol and diesel options, and new safety features like anti-lock brakes. Different models were available, including the sporty M535i. People liked its smooth ride, luxury interior, and strong build quality, making it a top choice in the executive sedan market.

BMW 5 Series (E28)

1981-1988

Nine years after its initial launch, BMW introduced the second generation of the 5 Series, known as the E28. It impressed with advanced technologies for its time.

The second generation of the 5 Series, the E28, maintained the design essence of its predecessor but came with significant technical upgrades. It was BMW’s first car with a turbodiesel engine and ABS brakes. Plus, it featured luxury amenities like air conditioning, power windows, and locks. The E28 also introduced the legendary M5 version. With its distinctive front shark nose and flat profile, the E28 boasted a revolutionary cabin design with a driver-oriented center stack and a comfortable rear bench seat. Under the hood, it offered various engine options, including the iconic M5 with impressive horsepower.

Third generation

The third generation of the BMW 5 Series, called the E34, was from 1988 to 1996. It had a sleeker design and better aerodynamics. There were many engine options, including inline-four, inline-six, and V8 engines, offering more power and efficiency. The E34 introduced new tech like traction control and stability control for better driving and safety. Models included the 518i, 520i, 525i, 530i, and the powerful 540i. People loved its quality build, luxury interior, and fun driving experience, making it a top choice in luxury sedans.

BMW 5 Series (E34)

1988-1995

BMW introduced the third generation of the 5 Series, the E34, in December 1987, just under eight years after the second generation's debut in 1981.

Italian designer Ercole Spada crafted the initial sketches for the third generation of the 5 Series in 1982, just a year after the second generation's debut. BMW aimed for a more radical overhaul this time, resulting in a longer, more comfortable car with more powerful engines. It was the first 5 Series to offer all-wheel drive and a station wagon variant from 1992. The E34 also saw the introduction of two M5 versions.

While it retained the four-headlight styling, the front grille featured BMW's signature kidney grille design. The body boasted curved lines for improved aerodynamics, with body-colored door handles and mirrors. Inside, the driver-focused layout included an angled center stack and a trapezoidal instrument cluster. The car offered various engine options, including a V8 for the top-end 540i model, along with adaptive dampers and traction control for enhanced performance and safety.

BMW 5 Series Touring (E34)

1992-1997

BMW launched the fourth generation of the 5 Series in late 1987, but it wasn't until 1991 that the station wagon version made its debut, hitting the market as a 1992 model.

The BMW 5 Series Touring, BMW's first station wagon based on the 5 Series, gained popularity for its sporty yet family-friendly design and ample cargo space. Offering options like all-wheel drive for the 2.5-liter gasoline engine and fuel-efficient turbodiesel engines, it impressed with speeds over 200 kph (124 mph). The Touring also boasted comfort and safety features, inheriting BMW's signature four-headlight styling and adopting a more curved body style compared to earlier models. Inside, the dashboard featured BMW's classic four-dial layout, and customers could choose fabric or leather upholstery. With a split-folding bench seat, the Touring provided versatility, expanding trunk space from 420 liters (14.8 cu-ft) to 1,670 liters (59 cu-ft). Engine options included inline-six and V8 engines, along with turbodiesel variants, although all-wheel drive was limited to the 2.5-liter gasoline engine.

Fourth generation

The fourth generation of the BMW 5 Series, called the E39, was made from 1995 to 2003. It had a sleek design and offered a variety of engines, including six-cylinder and V8 options. It introduced new technologies like Valvetronic and VANOS for better performance and fuel efficiency. Innovations like iDrive and Adaptive Headlights set new standards for luxury and driving experience. Model options included the 520i, 523i, 528i, 530i, 535i, 540i, and the high-performance M5. People loved its quality, refined interior, and enjoyable driving feel, making it a top choice in luxury sedans.

BMW 5 Series (E39)

1995-2000

In December 1995, BMW unveiled the fourth generation of the 5 Series, called the E39. It marked a significant leap forward in the model's history, boasting substantial improvements across the board.

The E39 generation of the 5 Series, introduced in the late 1990s, was a game-changer. It combined sportiness with luxury and refinement, appealing to executives and families alike. Unlike its predecessors, it shed weight thanks to aluminum suspension components and featured new, lighter engines, enhancing its agility. It ditched the four-headlight design for sleek dual lamps, contributing to its aerodynamic profile. Inside, it offered a spacious and feature-rich cabin, with options like a multifunction steering wheel and premium materials. Under the hood, it boasted powerful engines, including V8s and turbocharged diesels, delivering rear-wheel-drive performance.

BMW 5 Series Touring (E39)

1997-2000

Two years after launching the fourth generation of the 5 Series, BMW introduced its station wagon variant, known as the Touring.

When BMW introduced the E39 5 Series in 1995, it surprised customers with its unique front design, which eventually won appreciation. However, one downside was its limited luggage space, unlike its predecessor, the E34, which had a wagon version. This was rectified in 1997 with the second generation of the 5 Series Touring.

The E39's front featured dual headlamps under a single glass cover and an aerodynamic bumper with integrated fog lamps. Its profile shared body panels with the sedan up to the B-pillars, with new rear doors to maintain consistency. It boasted a sporty rear with a tailgate and an independent rear window.

Inside, the E39 showcased a dashboard with rounded corners and flowing lines. It housed a large instrument cluster with an onboard computer display. The front seats were bolstered, and the center console housed the gear stick. The rear bench seat was split-folding, offering ample trunk space.

Under the hood, BMW introduced all-aluminum engines for reduced weight. The E39 was the first BMW with fully aluminum suspension elements. Engine options included gasoline and turbodiesel paired with manual or automatic transmissions.

BMW 5 Series (E39)

2000-2003

In late 2000, BMW refreshed the fourth-generation 5 Series for the 2001 model year. Alongside exterior tweaks, the update included enhancements to the engines.

By 2000, the BMW 5 Series was already a top-selling luxury sedan globally, boasting a wide range of engines and advanced features. Though lacking all-wheel drive like some rivals, its engineering prowess impressed. The 2001 facelift, known as the LCI, refreshed its appearance and marked a milestone as the first 5 Series to surpass 1.5 million units sold.

At the front, notable changes included new headlights with circular daytime running lights, dubbed "angel eyes," and clear turn signals. The lower bumper featured a wider air intake and improved aerodynamics. From the side, a body-colored stripe replaced the black rubber one, contributing to a smoother profile. The curved roofline and iconic Hofmeister kink remained, maintaining BMW's signature style. At the rear, updated taillights added a modern touch.

Inside, upholstery and trim options ranged from cloth to leather with wood accents. A new steering wheel with stereo controls was introduced, and an optional M-Sport package offered a sportier feel. The center stack housed an updated infotainment display with a wider ratio.

Under the hood, BMW revamped the engine lineup to meet Euro 3 emission standards, offering both gasoline and diesel options. Most models came with five-speed manuals or automatics, while select versions offered a six-speed manual in certain markets.

Fifth generation

The fifth generation of the BMW 5 Series, known as the E60, was from 2003 to 2010. It had a futuristic design with bold lines. It offered various engines, including six-cylinder, V8, and V10 options, with features like High Precision Injection and Active Steering for better performance. The E60 introduced advanced technologies like Active Cruise Control and Night Vision for safety and luxury. Models included the 520i, 523i, 525i, 530i, 535i, 540i, and the M5. Despite mixed opinions on its looks, it was praised for its dynamic driving and high-tech features, reinforcing BMW's status in luxury sedans.

BMW 5 Series (E60)

2003-2007

BMW unveiled the fifth generation of the 5 Series in 2003, and the initial reaction to its design was negative.

In the early 1990s, BMW hired designer Chris Bangle to revamp its design language, but not everyone liked the bold new direction. The fifth generation of the 5 Series, the E60, faced criticism for its unconventional design, especially following the beloved E39. The controversial styling, including swept-back headlights and a double-bubble dashboard, led to slower sales compared to its predecessor. However, the E60 was praised for its advanced technology, smooth engines, and quality interiors. Despite the mixed reception, it introduced innovations like all-wheel drive options and features like active steering and adaptive headlights.

BMW 5 Series Touring (E61)

2004-2007

A year after unveiling the 5 Series E60, BMW introduced its station wagon counterpart, known as the Touring.

In 2004, BMW released the Touring version of the 5 Series E60, labeled as the E61. Despite sharing the sedan's flaws and unusual design, it aimed to compete with rivals like the Mercedes-Benz E-Class and Audi A6. The front sported distinctive swept-back headlights with "angel eyes" and a broad lower bumper. The E61 continued the tradition of sharing front parts with the sedan but extended behind the B-pillars to accommodate a spacious trunk. Inside, the dashboard design divided opinions, but the iDrive system offered convenient control. The rear seats folded for extra cargo space. Engine options included gasoline and turbodiesel, paired with manual or automatic transmissions, and some models featured all-wheel drive.

BMW 5 Series Touring (E61)

2007-2010

BMW introduced both the 5 Series Sedan and Touring versions simultaneously in 2004 with the facelifted fifth generation.

Three years after the debut of the fifth-gen 5 Series Touring, BMW hurried out a refreshed version to maintain the model despite sluggish sales. While the polarizing exterior design stayed mostly unchanged, there were subtle tweaks, like clearer headlights and revamped bumper details. Inside, there were more noticeable upgrades, with new trims and an improved iDrive system. The engine lineup also got a refresh to meet stricter emissions standards, offering a range of options paired with manual or automatic transmissions, and rear- or all-wheel drive configurations.

BMW 5 Series (E60)

2007-2009

In 2007, BMW updated the 5 Series (E60), but the changes didn't address the unconventional design choices of the original version, disappointing customers who were hoping for a more conventional look.

In 2007, BMW updated the 5 Series (E60) with minor exterior tweaks but focused more on enhancing the interior and adding new features. While the design remained largely unchanged, the front received new headlights with clear lenses and updated turn signal lamps. Inside, materials were improved, and the iDrive infotainment system was upgraded. The six-speed automatic gearbox lever was redesigned for better ergonomics. Under the hood, BMW refreshed the engine lineup to improve fuel efficiency and meet stricter emission standards.

Sixth generation

The sixth generation of the BMW 5 Series, known as the F10, was made from 2010 to 2016. It had a sleeker design with smoother lines. Engine options included four-cylinder, six-cylinder, V8, and hybrid variants for better fuel efficiency. New technologies like Lane Departure Warning and Collision Mitigation improved safety. Models included the 520i, 525i, 528i, 530i, 535i, 540i, and the ActiveHybrid 5. People liked its smooth ride, luxurious interior, and advanced features, making it a top choice in executive sedans.

BMW 5 Series (F10)

2009-2013

In late 2009, BMW debuted the sixth generation of its 5 Series sedan, the F10, in Germany, with a global rollout in 2010. The changes compared to its predecessor were significant.

In 2006, Jacek Fröhlich designed the seventh generation of the 5 Series, while Chris Bangle led BMW's styling. The new model, built on the CLAR platform like the 7 Series, was a hit, a departure from the bold E60. Its sleek headlights, kidney grille, and sculpted lines gave it a sporty yet elegant look. Inside, it boasted an integrated infotainment system and spacious seating. Under the hood, it offered a range of engines with manual or automatic transmission options, all delivering power to the rear wheels or available all-wheel drive.

BMW 5 Series Touring (F11)

2010-2013

In 2010, BMW added a station wagon version to the sixth generation of the 5 Series lineup, targeting families seeking more space and versatility.

At the 2010 Paris Motor Show, BMW revealed the Touring version of the 5 Series, boasting practicality and sportiness. While not the biggest in its class, its trunk impressed with functionality without sacrificing style. With top-notch technology and powerful engines, it overshadowed the absence of a dedicated M engine. Sporting similar front features as the sedan, its longer roofline and panoramic glass roof added flair. Inside, the premium cabin boasted an iDrive 7 system, while the spacious rear seat offered flexibility with a split-folding design. Engine options included both diesel and gasoline variants, with manual or automatic transmissions, and available all-wheel drive depending on the region.

BMW 5 Series Touring (F11) LCI

2013-2017

In 2013, BMW updated the entire sixth-generation 5 Series lineup, including the Touring version, to comply with the upcoming Euro 6 emission standards. This refresh ensured both the sedan and estate models were up to date with regulatory requirements.

In 2013, BMW refreshed the sixth-generation 5 Series, including the Touring version, without introducing new engines until the following year. However, they made significant upgrades elsewhere. The exterior saw subtle changes, including standard bi-xenon headlights and redesigned lower bumpers. The profile remained similar, but new door mirrors with integrated LED turn signals were added. The interior received a new infotainment system, the NBT, with improved functionality. Options included a 10.25-inch TFT display and a split-folding rear bench seat. Under the hood, a variety of engines were available, paired with either a six-speed manual or an eight-speed automatic transmission, and rear or all-wheel drive systems. Later, the N-family engines replaced the previous ones, offering slightly more power.

BMW 5 Series (F10) LCI

2013-2017

In 2013, BMW updated the sixth-generation 5 Series (F10), continuing changes initiated four years earlier.

BMW gave its popular 5 Series, the F10, some updates in 2013 and 2014. The exterior saw minor tweaks, like new headlights and alloy wheel options. Inside, the dashboard got a tech upgrade with a larger infotainment screen and advanced features like BMW ConnectedDrive. Safety features like adaptive cruise control and automatic emergency braking were also available. Under the hood, new engine options were introduced for improved fuel efficiency and lower emissions. Transmission options remained the same, with both manual and automatic choices, and power could be sent to either the rear or all wheels.

Seventh

The seventh generation of the BMW 5 Series, known as the G30, came out in 2017 and is still being made today. It has a sleek design with advanced technology and strong performance. Engine options range from four-cylinder to V8, and there are hybrid versions for better fuel efficiency. It features modern driver assistance systems, like semi-autonomous driving, and connectivity features such as gesture control and the latest iDrive infotainment system. Models include the 520i, 530i, 540i, and the high-performance M5. People love its smooth driving, luxurious interior, and top-notch technology, keeping BMW at the forefront of the luxury sedan market.

BMW 5 Series (G30)

2016-2020

After seven years, BMW replaced the sixth-generation 5 Series, the F10, with the G30 model. The G30 was more of an evolution than a revolution in terms of design.

The seventh generation of the 5 Series was unveiled in late 2016 and launched in 2017. Despite looking like a heavily facelifted version of its predecessor, the F10, it was actually a completely new model built on the CLAR platform from the 7 Series. It boasted standard LED headlights, an elegant profile with short overhangs, and a refined interior with a free-floating iDrive display. The G30 offered a range of engines, both gasoline and turbodiesel, paired with an eight-speed automatic gearbox and available in rear- or all-wheel drive configurations.

BMW 5 Series Touring (G31)

2017-2020

In February 2017, BMW introduced the station wagon version of the seventh generation 5 Series, shortly after unveiling its sedan counterpart at the North American International Auto Show.

When BMW first introduced the 5 Series in 1972, there was no station wagon option. It wasn't until the third generation, two decades later, that the wagon version arrived and became quite popular, especially with some M-powered versions. In 2017, BMW launched the G30 5 Series sedan and its G31 Touring counterpart, continuing the design language of the previous generation. Built on the CLAR platform shared with the 7 Series and Rolls-Royce Ghost, the new models featured standard LED headlights and a sleeker grille design. The Touring model retained the sporty estate look, with a slightly reduced trunk space for style. Inside, there was a new iDrive infotainment system with a 10.25-inch touchscreen and gesture control. The Touring model offered ample space with a split-folding rear seat, expanding trunk capacity up to 1,700 liters. Under the hood, a range of engines including turbodiesel, gasoline, and hybrid options were available, paired with an improved eight-speed automatic transmission.

BMW 5-Series Touring (G31) LCI

2020-2024

Just three years after its debut in February 2017, BMW gave the 5 Series Touring a refresh for the 2020 model year. This update brought noticeable improvements to the vehicle's appearance, interior, and drivetrain.

The seventh generation of the 5 Series, the G30/G31 lineup, was a hit for BMW, and they aimed to keep the momentum going with a refresh. Despite challenges like the 2020 pandemic and the microchip crisis, BMW went ahead with the update. The 2020 model featured slimmer LED headlights with L-shaped daytime running lights, and a broader kidney grille for a sleeker look. The cabin got a boost too, with options for a 12.3-inch driver display and an updated 10.25-inch touchscreen with Apple CarPlay and Android Auto. Under the hood, there were diesel, gasoline, and plug-in hybrid options, including the powerful 545e iPerformance with nearly 400 horsepower, capable of a rapid sub-five-second sprint to 62 mph.

BMW 5 Series (G30) LCI

2020-Present

BMW introduced the seventh generation of the 5 Series in late 2016, with deliveries starting in 2017. Four years later, in 2020, it received a refresh.

The seventh generation of the BMW 5 Series, known as the G30, was introduced in late 2016 and refreshed in 2020. It shared many features with the more expensive 7 Series but was more affordable and offered fuel-efficient inline-four engines. The 2020 update brought significant upgrades, including slimmer LED headlights, an option for laser technology, and redesigned taillights.

Inside, the G30 featured a 10.2-inch touchscreen for the iDrive system and an optional 12.3-inch display for the BMW Live Cockpit Professional. The front seats came in various materials, with options for a 40/20/40 split-folding rear bench, different sound systems, and multiple USB ports. Heated and ventilated front seats were also available.

Under the hood, the G30 offered a range of engines, including a base 520i with 184 PS, a 2.0-liter turbodiesel, mild-hybrid, and plug-in hybrid versions. Most models had an eight-speed automatic transmission, with options for all-wheel drive and rear-wheel steering. The G30 could serve as a cost-effective alternative to the 7 Series.

Eigth generation

The eighth generation of the BMW 5 Series, called the G60, debuted in 2023. It has a modern design with sharp lines and offers a range of engines, including hybrids. It features the latest driver assistance systems and an improved iDrive 8.0 infotainment system. Models include the 530i, 540i, and hybrid options, with both rear-wheel and all-wheel drive available. The G60 is praised for its technology, luxury, and driving experience, keeping BMW's high standards in the executive sedan market.

BMW 5 Series (G60)

2023-Present

BMW has introduced the new 2024 BMW 5 Series, continuing its legacy of seven generations since 1972. This premium vehicle is designed to deliver driving pleasure to people worldwide.

The 2024 BMW 5 Series offers a range of propulsion systems, including gasoline, diesel, mild hybrid, and a fully electric version (BMW i5). It features an 8-speed automatic Steptronic Sport transmission for smooth acceleration. With near-perfect 50:50 weight distribution, the 5 Series delivers impressive dynamics, available in both all-wheel drive (xDrive) and standard versions.

The front showcases modern headlights and a bean-shaped grille, optionally with BMW Iconic Glow lighting. At the back, L-shaped taillights with a chrome stripe highlight the car's design. The side view is familiar yet dynamic, enhanced by optional M Sport Packages.

The interior is fully vegan and highly technologized, featuring the BMW Curved Display and fewer buttons. The redesigned steering wheel has a flattened lower section, and the optional BMW Interaction Bar spans the width of the instrument panel.

Parking Assistant and Reversing Assist are standard, with optional Parking Assistant Professional for automated parking. The Highway Assistant, available in Canada, Germany, and the USA, includes Active Lane Change Assistant with eye confirmation, allowing hands-free driving up to 85 mph and lane change suggestions confirmed by looking in the mirror.

BMW 5 Series Touring (G60)

2024-Present

In early 2024, BMW expanded the eighth generation of the 5 Series by introducing the station wagon version, traditionally named Touring.

BMW introduced the first 5 Series in 1972 but didn't offer a station wagon version until 1992, which became popular for its innovative features like the opening rear window. Some versions also had an M treatment for high performance. In 2023, BMW launched the eighth generation of the 5 Series and announced the Touring version, which was released in early 2024.

The 2024 5 Series Touring shared front-end options with the sedan, including various grille trims and standard LED headlights, with an option for laser lights. It featured a raked-forward tailgate for a sportier look and matched the sedan's length. The gasoline and diesel models came with 18-inch wheels, while hybrids and electric versions had 19-inch wheels, with options up to 21 inches.

Inside, it had a curved display with two screens: one for the instrument cluster and one for the infotainment system running on BMW’s iDrive 8.5 OS. The trunk offered up to 570 liters (20.1 cu-ft) of space with the seats up and 1,700 liters (60 cu-ft) when folded, similar to the previous generation.

Under the hood, it offered gasoline and turbo diesel engines with mild-hybrid technology, along with plug-in hybrid (PHEV) versions available with rear or all-wheel drive. It also featured an adaptive pneumatic suspension with self-leveling.

Gran turismo

The BMW 5 Series Gran Turismo, or 5 Series GT, is a mix of sedan, wagon, and crossover. It has a unique hatchback design with lots of space inside. Introduced in 2009, it's great for long trips and carrying big loads. It comes with powerful engine options, including hybrids, and has a luxurious interior with advanced technology. While it's not like a regular sedan or SUV, the 5 Series GT offers a comfortable and versatile driving experience.

BMW 5 Series Gran Turismo

2009-2013

BMW introduced a niche vehicle called the 5 Series GT, which shared its platform with the 7 Series (F01).

BMW created the 5 Series GT by blending elements of its 7 Series limousine with the X6 Sport Activity Coupe, aiming to provide comfort for four passengers and ample luggage space. Despite offering richer features than the 7 Series at a lower price, its potential was limited by the 2008 financial crisis. The 5 Series GT had notable design features, such as a broad kidney grille, swept-back headlights, a tall front fascia, and a dual-opening tailgate inspired by the Skoda Superb. Inside, it boasted high-mounted seats, reclining rear seats, and a frameless door window design. Under the hood, it offered a range of engines from a 2.0-liter turbodiesel to a twin-turbo 4.4-liter V8, with rear-wheel or all-wheel drive and an eight-speed automatic transmission.

In 2013, BMW sought to enhance the sixth generation of the 5 Series, including its Gran Turismo variant, which struggled with lower sales compared to the sedan and estate versions.
