Fun and versatile

First generation

The first generation of the BMW 2 Series, launched in 2014, came as a coupe and a convertible, offering a sportier alternative to the 1 Series. It featured a sleek design, a high-quality interior, and the latest tech. With powerful turbocharged engines, the 2 Series delivered strong performance and excellent handling, making it fun to drive. It combined sporty driving dynamics with everyday practicality and luxury.

BMW 2 Series

2013-2017

Initially, BMW offered the sportier 1 Series Coupe based on the 1 Series hatchback. The 2 Series replaced the 1 Series Coupe, but BMW made more changes than just the name.

The 2013 BMW 2 Series, available only as a coupe, featured significant styling and mechanical upgrades, offering more interior room and cargo space.

With sharp headlights and redesigned taillights, it had an attractive coupe look with a saloon-style trunk. Inside, the high-quality, premium design included soft-touch materials and plenty of standard equipment. The cabin had ample storage, including cupholders and door bins, and the rear passenger space was spacious for a coupe, with a central armrest and cupholders.

Standard features included automatic climate control, USB and Bluetooth connectivity, a multi-function steering wheel, and rain-sensing wipers. Optional features included leather upholstery, parking sensors, and large alloy wheels.

The 2 Series offered various driving modes and a great suspension, providing a sporty driving experience. The M235i model, for thrill-seekers, could reach 100 km/h in 4.8 seconds with an 8-speed automatic transmission. All engines could be paired with a 6-speed manual transmission at no extra cost, though the automatic gearbox was standard.

BMW 2 Series Convertible

2014-2017

The BMW 2 Series is the only premium compact convertible on the market. Introduced in 2014, the open-top version added a soft-top, some weight, and extra charm.

The BMW 2 Series convertible features a seven-layer soft top for soundproofing and thermal insulation. It's electrically powered and retracts in 20 seconds. The car looks good with the roof up.

Inside, standard features include the iDrive and navigation system. However, the rear seats are cramped, suitable only for children, and unusable with the rear wind deflector installed.

The 2 Series offers various gasoline and diesel engines, paired with a 6-speed manual or 8-speed automatic transmission. The most powerful is the 326 hp M235i with a twin-scroll turbo engine.

Designed for open-air driving, the car allows front occupants to converse comfortably with the windows up and top down.

BMW 2 Series Convertible (F23) LCI

2017-Present

BMW introduced the first generation of the 2 Series as a coupe in 2013, followed by a convertible version in 2014. Both were refreshed in 2017.

The BMW 2 Series started as a re-badged 1 Series Coupe, renamed because BMW decided all coupes should have even numbers. The 2 Series Convertible shared many components with the 1 Series and received its last update before a new generation in 2019.

From 2017, the 2 Series featured bi-LED headlights and optional LED fog lights. The rear had standard LED L-shaped taillights. The updated interior included a new dashboard, a top-mounted infotainment screen, and an iDrive controller on the center console. Customers had a choice of new upholstery fabrics and leather designs.

The 2 Series Convertible offered eight engine options (136 hp to 340 hp) with either a 6-speed manual or 8-speed automatic transmission, and rear- or all-wheel drive.

BMW 2 Series (F22) LCI

2017-2021

In 2017, BMW introduced a facelifted version, known as LCI (Life Cycle Impulse), after four years on the market, along with a revised engine lineup.

After introducing the 1 Series in 2004, BMW realized the coupe appealed to different customers than the hatchback, leading to the creation of the 2 Series as a sportier, less practical two-door coupe or convertible. The 2017 model featured a restyled exterior and upgraded interior, making it more appealing.

The front fascia received new bi-LED headlights and hexagonal daytime running lights. The M-package offered wider air scoops to cool the front brakes, new side skirts, and L-shaped LED taillights.

Inside, the 2 Series offered fabric bucket seats or leather sports seats, an analog instrument cluster with a color display, and an 8.8” infotainment screen controlled by the iDrive system. The upgraded infotainment unit included BMW ConnectedDrive, real-time traffic information, and Apple CarPlay connectivity. The rear bench seat was cramped, and the trunk held 390 liters (13.7 cu-ft.) of storage.

Under the hood, BMW offered seven turbocharged engines (gasoline and diesel) with three, four, or six cylinders, paired with six-speed manuals or eight-speed automatics. Some models, like the 220i, 230i, 225d, and M240i xDrive Coupe, were only available with automatics. Customers could choose between rear or all-wheel-drive systems depending on the engine and options.

Second generation

The second generation of the BMW 2 Series, launched in 2021, features a sharper, more modern design and an updated front grille. Inside, it has better materials and advanced tech for a more luxurious feel. The car offers powerful engines, including four-cylinder and six-cylinder options, delivering great performance. It also comes with enhanced safety and driver assistance features. Overall, it combines sporty driving, modern looks, and advanced technology.

BMW 2 Series Coupe (G42)

2021-Present

BMW unveiled the 2022 2 Series Coupe at the 2021 Goodwood Festival of Speed. Unlike its front-wheel-drive hatchback sibling, the two-door version retained its traditional rear-wheel-drive system.

BMW changed its naming system, using even numbers for coupes and odd numbers for four- or five-door models. Thus, the 1 Series Coupe became the 2 Series, retaining RWD or AWD.

The 2 Series Coupe featured a unique front fascia with a flat, broad grille and slim LED headlights. Wider tires fit its enlarged fenders, with options up to 255/35 R 19 rear and 225/40 R 19 front.

Inside, it offered 2021 technology, including Android Auto, Apple CarPlay, and an 8.8" standard touch-screen (with a 10.25" optional). A 12.3" digital instrument panel displayed car info, navigation, and music. It had front bucket seats, a rear bench with limited space, and a 390-liter trunk.

Under the hood, it had gasoline and turbo-diesel engine options: two inline-four engines (one gasoline, one turbo-diesel) and a turbocharged inline-six.

Tourer

The BMW 2 Series Active Tourer, introduced in 2014, is a compact family car with front-wheel drive for more space inside. It has a roomy interior with adjustable rear seats for extra storage. The car features high-quality materials, modern design, and advanced technology. It offers various efficient engines, including petrol, diesel, and hybrid options. The 2 Series Active Tourer combines BMW's luxury with practicality, making it perfect for families.

BMW 2 Series Active Tourer (F45)

2014-2018

The 2 Series Active Tourer, introduced in 2014, was BMW's first front-wheel-drive car, causing controversy. BMW fans would have considered a front-wheel-drive MPV by BMW a joke before its release.

In 2014, BMW introduced the 2 Series Active Tourer, marking its first venture into front-wheel-drive cars. This move combined the practicality of an MPV with BMW's sporty driving experience, causing quite a stir among automotive enthusiasts. The 2 Series Active Tourer featured a high driving position and seven seats, but also packed a surprising 231 hp 2.0-liter engine.

Designed with versatility in mind, the interior included sliding and foldable rear seats (configured 40:20:40), ample storage spaces, and a refreshed center console. The vehicle also introduced a new generation of modular engines, starting as small as 1.5 liters.

All engines at launch met the latest EU6 CO2 emissions standards and were paired with either new 6-speed automatic or manual gearboxes, or the more common 8-speed ZF automatic unit. The 2 Series Active Tourer made its official debut at the 2014 Geneva Motor Show. This model was ideal for those who appreciated BMW's driving dynamics but needed more room and practicality.

BMW 2 Series Gran Tourer (F45)

2015-2018

The BMW 2-Series Gran Tourer was BMW's first modern attempt to enter the small MPV market, offering more room and luxury in a premium vehicle.

The Gran Tourer was the seven-seat version of the 2014 BMW 2-Series Active Tourer. It was more related to the MINI hatchback and featured a front-wheel-drive architecture instead of the rear-wheel drive of the regular 2-Series.

The front design included the classic kidney grille but had calmer headlights. The raked A-pillars and longer roofline gave it a typical MPV profile. The interior used high-quality materials similar to the 2-Series, with a flat dashboard and an optional 6.5” color display for the infotainment system, controlled by the iDrive knob. The last row of seats was foldable and located in the trunk area.

The 2-Series Gran Tourer offered front or all-wheel drive and a range of engines with three or four cylinders, producing between 116 hp and 192 hp. It did not have a plug-in hybrid option.

BMW 2 Series Gran Tourer (F46)

2018-Present

The first generation of the 2-Series Gran Tourer was introduced in June 2015, shocking BMW fans again with its front-wheel-drive design.

BMW management supported the 2-Series Gran Tourer project, knowing the market needed a small vehicle that could carry more people. Competing with Mercedes-Benz's A-Class and B-Class, the Gran Tourer offered enough room for five adults and two children due to its Mini-based platform with a transverse engine.

The facelift included a new front fascia with a larger grille and new headlights. Inside, the center stack was driver-focused, with new materials and a BMW M-Performance package. Longer seat cushions added comfort, and trim levels included Luxury Line, Sport Line, and M Sport packages.

The powertrain options expanded to include three- and four-cylinder engines and a plug-in hybrid, with manual or automatic transmissions, including a new 7-speed dual-clutch option.

BMW 2 Series Active Tourer (F45)

2018-2021

The first generation of the 2-Series Active Tourer was introduced at the 2014 Geneva Motor Show, shocking BMW fans with its front-wheel-drive layout.

BMW traditionally featured rear or all-wheel-drive systems. The introduction of a front-wheel-drive system in a BMW was difficult for fans to accept. However, the 2-Series Active Tourer saw high sales, prompting a facelift in 2018.

Despite its compact size, the 2-Series Active Tourer offered ample space for five adults. Sharing a platform with the Mini and using a transverse engine, the designers maximized cabin space. The facelift included a new front fascia with a larger grille and updated headlights.

Inside, the center stack was driver-oriented. New materials and a BMW M-Performance package enhanced the interior. Longer seat cushions improved comfort on long trips. Trim levels included Luxury Line, Sport Line, and M Sport packages.

The facelift also brought a range of three- and four-cylinder engines, plus a plug-in hybrid. Transmission options included a manual, an automatic, and a new 7-speed dual-clutch.

BMW 2 Series Active Tourer

2021-Present

The BMW 2 Series Active Tourer, a small-segment minivan, provided a spacious interior suitable for families, all while maintaining the premium quality associated with the manufacturer.

The BMW 2 Series Active Tourer sported a front fascia with a broad grille, giving it a larger appearance despite its compact size. Its design hinted at SUV elements, with a raked windshield and a long greenhouse, while the sides resembled a stretched 2 Series hatchback.

Inside, the dashboard boasted clean lines and a minimalist design. The car featured a digital instrument cluster and a touch-screen infotainment system atop the center stack, with ample storage space including a wireless phone charger. The gearbox controls were relocated to the center armrest, prioritizing family-friendly usability with ample legroom for all passengers.

Under the hood, BMW offered a wide range of engine options, including gasoline, turbodiesel, and hybrid models, with plug-in hybrid versions introduced from 2022. The 2 Series Active Tourer came standard with a seven-speed automatic (dual-clutch) gearbox across the range, with no manual option available.

Grand Coupé

The BMW 2 Series Gran Coupe, introduced in 2019, is a stylish compact sedan with a sleek design. It features a high-quality interior with modern tech, like a digital dashboard. It has powerful, efficient engines and some models offer all-wheel drive. The 2 Series Gran Coupe combines sporty looks, luxury, and practicality.

BMW 2 Series Gran Coupe (F44)

2019-Present

In 2019, BMW launched a four-door version of the 2 Series, built on the Mini platform, aiming to provide a compact yet luxurious vehicle.

BMW debuted the four-door coupe variant of the 2 Series at the 2019 Los Angeles Auto Show, sharing its platform with the Mini and the 2 Series Active Tourer. Unlike its rear-wheel-drive sibling, this model targeted a younger, sportier audience with powerful engines.

Outside, it blended design cues from the luxurious 8 Series Gran Coupe and the 2 Series Active Tourer, sporting BMW's signature kidney grille and LED headlights. Inside, it featured a digital instrument cluster and a 10.25” infotainment screen with gesture control. The cabin offered ample space, with options for sports seats and a split-folding bench in the rear.

Engine options included transversely mounted diesel and gasoline engines, paired with six-speed manuals or eight-speed automatics. Front- or all-wheel-drive systems were available, with the M235i xDrive boasting a torque-sensing differential on the front axle for enhanced performance.
