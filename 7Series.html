First generation

The first generation of the BMW 7 Series, called the E23, was made from 1977 to 1986. It was BMW's first full-size luxury sedan, known for its elegant design and advanced features like an onboard computer and climate control. The E23 had a spacious interior and powerful inline-six engines, offering smooth performance. It set high standards for luxury and driving pleasure, paving the way for future BMW 7 Series models.

BMW 7 Series (E23)

1977-1986

The first generation of the BMW 7-Series, launched in 1977, followed the New Six concept introduced in 1968 with the E3 bodywork.

The first BMW 7-Series, launched in 1977, featured only six-cylinder engines and had a modern design with an angled "shark nose" fascia. Although a prototype with a V12 engine was created, it remained unique. The design moved away from previous trends, keeping only the four rounded headlights. The car had a flat, wide hood, a simple greenhouse, and a descending trunk lid, with chromed bumpers. In the 1983 facelift, the kidney grille was enlarged, and new plastic-covered bumpers were added.

Inside, the 7-Series introduced innovations like service-control lights, climate control, and an onboard computer. The base model had cloth seats and a manual gearbox, while higher trims included a sunroof, power windows, leather seats, and power locks. Initially, the engines had four-barrel Solex carburetors, but later all were upgraded to Bosch fuel injection systems. The most powerful version, the 286 hp 754i, featured an inline-six engine with a turbocharger.

Second generation

The second generation of the BMW 7 Series, the E32, was produced from 1986 to 1994. It featured a sleek design and introduced advanced technology like integrated telephones, double glazing, and the first V12 engine in a post-war German car. It also had safety features like electronic stability and traction control. Known for its luxurious interior and impressive performance, the E32 solidified the 7 Series as a top luxury sedan.

BMW 7 Series (E32)

1986-1994

The BMW 7-Series E32, introduced in 1986, was the first V12 car produced in Germany since WWII and was the most technologically advanced vehicle on the market at that time.

The battle for the luxury segment between Mercedes-Benz and BMW intensified when BMW introduced the second generation of the 7-Series in June 1986. Initially powered by an inline-six engine, the lineup later expanded to include V8 and V12 options.

Design work began in 1979, and the final project was ready by 1984, but production started in 1986. Over 300,000 units were produced until April 1994.

The 7-Series featured a driver-oriented cabin, making it popular among executives who drove their own cars. It included technological advancements like Automatic Stability Control (ASC) and a driver-tilted central console. For rear passengers, a long-wheelbase version offered ample legroom.

Under the hood, there were various engine choices. The V12 was known for performance, while the six-cylinder units were praised for fuel efficiency, especially with the 5-speed manual transmission.

Third generation

The third generation of the BMW 7 Series, known as the E38, was made from 1994 to 2001. It was a big step up in luxury, technology, and performance for BMW's flagship sedan. The E38 had a sleek design and introduced innovative features like the iDrive system for controlling vehicle functions and Active Comfort Seats for improved long-distance comfort. It offered powerful V8 and V12 engines for smooth performance. Loved for its luxury, advanced tech, and refined driving feel, the E38 set a high standard in the luxury sedan market.

BMW 7 Series (E38)

1994-1998

The third generation of the BMW 7-Series was highly sought after and is considered by many fans to be the best 7-Series ever made. It bridged the gap between old technology and the digital era.

The E38 generation was the last BMW 7-Series available with a manual transmission and the first with a V12 engine. It combined comfort, luxury, and sportiness, and was notably fuel-efficient with the 2.5-liter diesel engine (not available in North America).

The car had a low height similar to the Jaguar XJ series, dual headlamps with halogen or Xenon lights, and came in short or long wheelbase versions. An extra-long wheelbase model, the L7, was available for state and government officials.

Inside, it had five seats, but only two rear passengers could sit comfortably due to the low cabin height and high transmission tunnel. Options included various sound systems and an optional telephone.

Engine choices ranged from a 2.5-liter turbodiesel to a 5.4-liter V8. Most versions offered a 5- or 6-speed manual transmission, with a 5-speed automatic available for all models.

BMW 7 Series (E38)

1998-2001

The refreshed third-generation BMW 7-Series was released in 1998 with improved features and a more sophisticated design.

The 1998 facelift of the third-generation BMW 7-Series included revised headlights, new taillights, sportier suspension, new wheels, and a torque converter. BMW also introduced a new sports package, initially for the 740i, later available for 740iL and 750iL variants. The 728i engines were upgraded with variable valve timing.

New features included automatic climate control, front side airbags, a standard Head Protection System, and DSC. The facelift also added "Active Comfort Seats" for fatigue reduction and made head airbags standard.

The 740iL's 4.4-liter V8 engine had increased torque to 440 Nm. The 7-Series offered a generous 500 L trunk space, reliability, comfort, and luxury, making it ideal for long family trips. The E38 7-Series was discontinued in 2001, replaced by the fourth-generation model.

Fourth generation

The fourth generation BMW 7 Series, the E65/E66, was produced from 2001 to 2008. It featured a bold new design by Chris Bangle and introduced the iDrive infotainment system. It had powerful V8 and V12 engines and advanced technologies like adaptive headlights and a six-speed automatic transmission. Despite mixed reactions to its design, it was praised for its innovative tech, luxury, and performance.

BMW 7 Series (E65/E66)

2001-2005

The fourth-generation BMW 7-Series was both highly criticized for its look and widely loved. Despite the mixed reactions, it outsold its predecessors.

The E65 BMW 7-Series, designed by Chris Bangle, introduced a new "flame-surfacing" style, making the car look different from various angles. Though it appeared bulkier than its predecessor, it was the most technologically advanced car of its time, even winning an award in Japan.

The E65/E66 had a larger grille and a front bumper designed to highlight the headlights. The rear quarter-panel's arch did not align with the trunk lid. Inside, it featured a revolutionary infotainment system controlled by a rotary knob, developed with Nokia, and a double-bulged dashboard.

The long-wheelbase version (E66) offered ample legroom in the back. Available with a range of gasoline and diesel engines from inline-six to V12, it was the first BMW 7-Series to come exclusively with an automatic transmission.

BMW 7 Series (E65/E66)

2005-2007

In 2005, BMW refreshed its flagship 7 Series, updating its look, improving the powertrain, and making it Euro 4 compliant.

Despite its polarizing design, the BMW 7 Series (E65/E66) became the best-selling 7 Series ever. Some attribute its success to the advanced technologies, while others believe the bold design attracted new customers who no longer favored the classic look.

To address criticism of Chris Bangle's design, BMW updated the 7 Series' appearance. The front headlamp design was softened, and the rear end was smoothed out with changes to the taillights and panel.

Inside, BMW introduced an upgraded iDrive infotainment system with more features, including Bluetooth and navigation. The system's rotary knob allowed access to numerous functions. Paddle shifters were added to the steering wheel, and the long-wheelbase version (E66) offered business-class legroom.

BMW also enhanced the engine lineup, including a 4.8-liter engine from the X5 4.8is, paired with a six-speed automatic transmission across the range.

Fifth generation

The fifth generation BMW 7 Series, the F01/F02 (2008-2015), focused on luxury, technology, and performance. It had a refined design, advanced features like a new iDrive system, night vision, and adaptive cruise control. With a range of powerful engines and comfort-focused options like air suspension, it set high standards in luxury sedan comfort and innovation.

BMW 7 Series (F01/02)

2008-2012

The fifth generation of BMW's flagship sedan, the 7-Series, debuted in 2008 amidst the global economic crisis.

The fifth generation of BMW's flagship sedan, the 7-Series, debuted in 2008 with a new naming convention: F01/F02. It marked a departure from the controversial "Bangle" design of its predecessor, opting for a more traditional aesthetic. The design featured headlights integrated with the bumper, distinct kidney grilles, and long, straight lines emphasizing its length.

Inside, the 7-Series introduced a new interior with horizontal dashboard lines extending to the door panels. It boasted advanced technology including an evolved iDrive system and a sophisticated instrument cluster combining analog and digital displays. The car was equipped with up to five cameras for enhanced driver assistance, including infrared and traffic sign recognition. The spacious interior, especially in the long-wheelbase variant, offered ample room for passengers.

Under the hood, the F01/F02 7-Series offered turbocharged gasoline and diesel engines paired with rear or all-wheel-drive through an 8-speed automatic transmission.

BMW 7 Series (F01/02) Facelift

2012-2016

Since its debut in 1977, BMW's 7 Series has remained true to its core concept: a luxury car designed for the driver.

In 2008, BMW launched the fifth generation of the 7 Series, marking a departure from the Chris Bangle era with a new look and updated technology, transforming it into a luxurious and fast limousine that offered comfort both for drivers and rear-seat passengers.

By 2012, a mid-life refresh was necessary to meet new pollution norms and integrate new technologies into BMW's flagship vehicle. The exterior changes included new LED headlights, a redesigned bumper, and a radiator grille with fewer slats. At the rear, the LED taillights were connected by a chromed strip.

Inside, significant changes were seen with an optional 10.25" black screen replacing analog gauges in the instrument cluster. This allowed for customizable interior lighting themes corresponding to different driving modes selected by the driver.

For rear-seat passengers, depending on trim levels and options, control over music and navigation systems was possible via an iDrive rotary knob located in the center armrest. The new 750d xDrive and BMW 730d models were equipped to meet Euro 6 emission standards.

Sixth generation

The sixth generation BMW 7 Series, the G11/G12 (since 2015), is known for its modern design and advanced technology. It features BMW's iconic kidney grille and sleek lines for better aerodynamics. Inside, it offers luxurious materials and innovative features like gesture control and laser headlights. With powerful engine options and all-wheel drive available, it combines luxury with high-performance capabilities, making it a top choice in the luxury sedan market.

BMW 7 Series (G11/G12)

2016-2019

In 2015, the BMW 7 Series made a bold return in its new generation as a 2016 model. It became the first 7 Series built on the CLAR platform, which incorporates a carbon-fiber-reinforced polymer chassis, similar to BMW's i Series models like the i8.

The 2016 BMW 7 Series underwent a major redesign, featuring updated exterior elements like new bumpers, larger kidney grille with integrated laser headlights, revised taillights, and new exhaust tips, rims, and paint choices. Inside, it boasts a revamped cabin with redesigned seats, dashboard, and advanced technology, particularly geared towards rear passengers.

Key highlights include a standard LED "light carpet" for easy entry, ambient LED lighting, iDrive 5 with touchscreen and Gesture Control, and luxurious amenities such as wood-trimmed seatbelts, multi-contour seats, heated armrests, and a Bowers & Wilkins Diamond Surround System. The panoramic roof, adorned with 15,000 LEDs, creates a starry sky effect.

Under the hood, new engines deliver enhanced power and efficiency, paired with an updated 8-speed automatic transmission. The use of CFRP and aluminum reduces weight, complemented by a 2-axle self-leveling air suspension. Available in rear-wheel-drive or all-wheel-drive (xDrive), engine options range from inline-6 cylinders to V8s, including diesel and gasoline choices, and a plug-in hybrid BMW 740e producing 326 hp. Later, a 12-cylinder M760i was added to the lineup.

Safety is comprehensive with knee airbags, rear-seat side airbags, active head restraints, rearview camera, and parking sensors. Driver assistance features like Attention Assist, adaptive headlights, and the Driver Assistance Plus Package enhance safety with surround-view cameras, forward collision warning, adaptive cruise control, active lane-keep assist, Side Collision Avoidance, and Traffic Jam Assistant for semi-autonomous driving.

Overall, the G11/G12 series blends efficiency, style, safety, and impressive performance, solidifying its place in the luxury car market.

BMW 7 Series (G11) LCI

2019-2022

The BMW 7 Series received a mid-life refresh in 2019 with extensive upgrades inside and out.

The 2019 BMW 7 Series LCI (Life Cycle Impulse) received extensive upgrades across its exterior, interior, and engine lineup.

Externally, it featured a new hood, front fenders, headlights, and grille. The addition of air breathers on the front fenders helped in reducing aerodynamic lift. At the rear, a full-width LED light strip was introduced.

Inside, luxury was enhanced with options for two wheelbases, offering more legroom for rear passengers. The facelifted model offered full Nappa leather and BMW Individual Merino full leather with extended quilting.

Under the hood, the engine and transmission options were revised. The hybrid version introduced a new xDrive system as an option, while the 8-speed automatic transmission received a launch-control mode. The lineup included the 6.0-liter V12 engine with all-wheel-drive and a potent 4.4-liter V8 unit with twin-scroll turbochargers between the cylinder banks, producing 530 hpâ€”an increase of 80 hp over its predecessor. The 750i xDrive could accelerate from 0 to 100 kph (0-62 mph) in just 4 seconds.

BMW 7 Series (G70)

2022-Present

BMW debuted the seventh generation of its flagship model, the 7 Series, with a bold new design that sparked attention worldwide. Alongside its striking appearance, the car impressed with cutting-edge technologies that delighted enthusiasts and drivers alike.

After nearly 50 years since the first 7 Series debuted, BMW launched its seventh generation, packed with innovations, including its first fully electric version to rival Mercedes-Benz's EQS.

The 2022 model turned heads with its daring design. It boasted the largest and widest grille ever seen on a BMW flagship, flanked by high-set LED daytime running lights and deeply embedded headlights. The rear featured a reinterpreted Hofmeister kink, adding to its distinctive look.

Inside, the cabin showcased a 12.3" digital instrument cluster and a 14.9" touchscreen infotainment system, both seamlessly integrated under a single continuous glass surface. A standard Panoramic Sky Lounge LED roof enhanced the luxury feel, complemented by options like a 31.3" 8K touchscreen with Amazon Fire TV.

Under the hood, it offered three engine choices: a new twin-turbo inline-six with mild-hybrid tech, a potent 4.4-liter twin-turbo V8 in the 760i, and the groundbreaking i7 xDrive60, BMW's first all-electric 7 Series model.
